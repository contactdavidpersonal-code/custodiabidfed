## The thesis

The internet has thousands of pages about CMMC. Almost all of them are written **by compliance people, for compliance people, about Level 2.** A small DoD contractor who has never thought about cybersecurity reads them and walks away more confused, more afraid, and convinced they need to hire a $200/hr consultant.

Custodia owns the opposite position: **the plain-English resource for the contractor who never asked to become a cybersecurity expert.** We translate the regulation, we kill the confusion, and we hand them an email list that brings them new contract leads every Monday — leads they now qualify for *because* they got CMMC L1 done.

This document is the playbook for becoming **the cited source** in Google AI Overviews, ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, and Gemini for every CMMC Level 1 question that exists.

## The reader we are writing for

Every post is written to **one specific reader**. If a sentence wouldn't make sense to her, it gets cut.

> **Maria, 47, owns a 12-person electrical contracting firm outside Norfolk, VA.** She just won her first DoD subcontract — wiring a hangar at NAS Oceana. Her prime emailed her a "supplier cybersecurity questionnaire." She doesn't have an IT department. Her son-in-law set up the office network in 2019. She's read the FAR clause four times and still doesn't know what it means. She is terrified she's going to lose this contract over something she doesn't understand.

**Maria assumes:**
- "Cybersecurity" means hackers in hoodies, not business hygiene.
- The federal government already has cybersecurity (because it's the government), so why does *she* have to do this?
- This is a new tax / regulatory burden someone invented to slow her down.
- She is too small to be a target.
- If she gets it wrong, she'll go to jail.

**Maria does not know:**
- The difference between FCI and CUI.
- That "Level 1" means something specific and bounded (only 15 things).
- That she can do this herself in a week.
- That getting CMMC L1 *opens up* new contracts she couldn't bid on before.

**Every blog post starts where Maria starts.** No jargon in the first 300 words. No assumed knowledge. The phrase "as you know" is banned.

## Voice rules — the rewrite that beats every other CMMC blog

1. **Frame compliance as a business upgrade, not a burden.** "You're not adding a tax. You're earning a credential that lets you bid on contracts your competitors can't."
2. **Re-frame "cybersecurity" as "business hygiene."** Locks on doors. Sign-in sheets. Knowing who has the office keys. Not hackers in hoodies.
3. **Explain *why now*.** The DoD lost $600B+ in stolen intellectual property to nation-state hackers (F-35 plans, missile designs, sub propulsion). The supply chain is the back door. Maria's firm is the supply chain. CMMC is how DoD plugs the door.
4. **Tell the truth about fear.** Acknowledge it. "Yes, getting it wrong has legal consequences. No, this isn't a trap to put small businesses in jail. Here's exactly what the senior official affirmation says and what it doesn't."
5. **No fake confidence.** Don't claim "easy in 5 minutes." Claim "boring in 3–5 days, then done for the year."
6. **One regulatory citation per claim.** Every factual statement carries a footnote to 32 CFR Part 170, FAR 52.204-21, the DoD CMMC Scoping Guide v2.13, or NIST SP 800-171A. AI engines weight this heavily.
7. **End every post the same way.** Two CTAs: (a) take the free `/cmmc-check` 5-min diagnostic, (b) subscribe to the Monday Bid Digest. *Always both. Never one.*

## Technical AEO/SEO setup — the plumbing

The content doesn't rank if the plumbing is broken. These are the changes to ship **before** publishing the next post.

### 1. Crawler permissions
- Audit `src/app/robots.ts` to explicitly **allow** `GPTBot`, `ClaudeBot`, `Claude-Web`, `PerplexityBot`, `Google-Extended`, `CCBot`, `Applebot-Extended`, `Amazonbot`, `Bytespider`, `Gemini`. (Currently allows `*`, which is fine in principle, but **explicit allow rules** prevent accidental future regressions and signal intent to crawlers.)
- Add `/blog`, `/sprs-check`, `/cmmc-check` to the explicit `allow` list.

### 2. `/llms.txt` at the root
- Publish a flat-text file at `/llms.txt` listing the 20 most-citable pages with one-line summaries. This is the emerging standard for inviting LLM crawlers to a curated subset of authoritative content. Free, fast, high upside.

### 3. Schema.org markup on every post

| Schema | Where | Why |
|---|---|---|
| `Article` | every post | baseline |
| `FAQPage` | posts with Q&A sections | AI engines extract FAQs verbatim |
| `HowTo` | procedural posts (T1.6, T1.7) | step extraction |
| `DefinedTerm` | T1.2, T1.4 | definitions get quoted |
| `BreadcrumbList` | every post | crawl efficiency |
| `Person` (Author) | every byline | E-E-A-T |
| `Organization` | global | E-E-A-T |

Add `@graph`-style JSON-LD with all schemas in one `<script>` per post.

### 4. Author authority
- Every post bylined by a real Custodia officer with credentials (CISSP / Security+ / CCP / CCA / etc.) and a linked bio page at `/team/<name>`.
- Add `Person` schema with `sameAs` linking to public LinkedIn profiles.

### 5. Citation hygiene
- Every factual claim links to a primary source: **32 CFR Part 170**, **FAR 52.204-21**, **DoD CMMC Scoping Guide v2.13**, **NIST SP 800-171A**, **DFARS 252.204-7019/7020/7021**, **48 CFR Part 204** (DFARS amendment).
- Cited regulations open in new tabs with `rel="noopener nofollow"`.
- We already have official docs in the project (`docs/`); we should curate a `/regulations` reference index page that becomes the canonical hub for primary sources.

### 6. Structured first paragraphs ("AEO lead")
Every post opens with a **50-word answer** to the post's question. That paragraph is the chunk AI engines will quote. Pattern:

> **CMMC Level 1 is the DoD's lowest cybersecurity requirement, mandatory for any contractor handling Federal Contract Information (FCI). It requires you to meet 15 safeguarding requirements from FAR 52.204-21, self-assess annually, and have a senior official affirm the result in SPRS. There is no score — it is binary.**

Then the rest of the post unpacks that.

### 7. Internal linking strategy
- Every Tier 1 post links to its pillar (Tier 0).
- Every Tier 2 post links to its closest Tier 1.
- Pillars link to all related posts.
- Sidebar: "**Related plain-English explainers**" with 3–4 links.
- Footer of every post: link to `/cmmc-check`, `/sprs-check`, `/pricing`, **and the Monday Bid Digest signup**.

### 8. Sitemap
- Already exists at `src/app/sitemap.ts`. Confirm every published post is auto-listed with `lastModified` reflecting the latest update.
- Submit to Google Search Console + Bing Webmaster Tools.

### 9. Page performance
- Blog posts must score **95+ on Lighthouse mobile**. AI engines deprioritize slow pages.
- Use `next/image` for all visuals with explicit width/height to prevent CLS.
- Defer all non-critical JS (motion, analytics).

### 10. Freshness signals
- Every post displays "**Last updated: [date]**" visibly near the title.
- Quarterly refresh on every Tier 0 + Tier 1 post: re-verify regulations, update dates, add 1–2 paragraphs of new context. Bump `dateModified`.

### 11. Get crawled fast
- Manual submit to Google Search Console **every new post** for 90 days. Forces priority crawl.
- Same for Bing Webmaster Tools.
- IndexNow for Bing (one HTTP call per new URL).
- Submit RSS to Google News (we already publish a category-aware RSS).

### 12. External authority signals
- **Wikipedia mention** on the CMMC article (one attempt with neutral phrasing + secondary source).
- **Cyber AB Marketplace listing** as a CMMC ecosystem tool.
- **One guest column / quote** in: GovCon Wire, Federal News Network, Washington Technology, Defense Daily. Each external mention with a backlink to a foundational post is worth ~50 internal links.
- **Reddit r/govcon presence** — answer real questions, link to foundational posts when relevant, don't spam. Use a real Custodia officer account.

## What we are explicitly NOT doing in this plan

- **No Level 2 content.** Every time we publish about L2 we dilute the L1 brand. Stay scoped.
- **No "CMMC certification" content.** L1 is self-affirmed; the word "certification" misleads our reader. Reserve "certification" for the L2 vendors to fight over.
- **No fear-bait headlines.** ("Are you about to lose your DoD contract?!") This audience trusts plain-spoken authority. Selling fear erodes the brand.
- **No gated content.** No "download our whitepaper to read." Every post is fully readable. The CTA is the trial, not the PDF.
- **No paid backlink schemes.** Slow, organic authority compounds. Black-hat link buying gets indexed and then deindexed.
